# Olivia Arcana — Custom Tarot Deck Concepts

> Five tarot deck designs optimized for digital delivery via Telegram (portrait, ~800x1400px). Each deck has a distinct visual world but maintains Olivia Arcana's brand quality. The deck is the visual centerpiece of the product — it must feel premium, distinctive, and screenshot-worthy.

---

## Format Specifications (All Decks)

- **Card dimensions:** 800 x 1400 px (portrait, ~1:1.75 ratio)
- **Safe area:** 40px margins on all sides (content within 720 x 1320 px)
- **Card name zone:** Top 120px reserved for card title
- **Bottom brand zone:** Bottom 80px for "Olivia Arcana" wordmark
- **File format:** PNG with transparency (back design) or JPEG at 95% quality (faces)
- **Telegram display:** Cards will be sent as photo messages — must be legible at 400px wide (half-res preview)

## Deck 1: "Arcana Aurea" (The Golden Arcana)

### Concept
Luxurious black-and-gold tarot with Art Deco geometry. Every card is a monument — bold gold line art on pitch-black backgrounds. The figures are stylized, elongated, Klimt-meets-Erté. No photorealism — pure decorative illustration.

**Art direction:** Flat gold vector illustration on black. Thick gold outlines, geometric fills (hatching, dots, chevrons). Figures are androgynous and elegant. No gradients — pure gold on black with occasional deep blue accents.

**Brand alignment:** Variant A (Celestial Noir)

### Major Arcana Samples

**0 — The Fool**
A figure stands at the edge of a geometric cliff — their body made of Art Deco arcs and circles in gold line art. They hold a small star in their right hand, arm extended toward a void of deep cosmic blue. A small dog (geometric, angular, fox-like) nips at their heel. Behind them, a golden sun rendered as a radiating circle of concentric lines. The cliff edge is a sharp diagonal, the void below filled with tiny gold dots suggesting distant stars. The figure's face is a simple oval with closed eyes — serene, not afraid. Their clothing is a series of overlapping geometric shapes like armor made of zodiac symbols.

**II — The High Priestess**
A seated figure centered between two gold pillars — one marked with a crescent moon (B), one with a full circle sun (J). She holds an open scroll across her lap rendered as a long horizontal gold rectangle with tiny constellation patterns engraved on it. Behind her: a veil made of repeating geometric moon phase symbols (crescent → half → full → half → crescent) in horizontal rows. A pomegranate tree rendered as a symmetrical gold pattern frames the top corners. Her crown is a triple moon symbol (crescent-circle-crescent) sitting above her geometric face. The overall composition is perfectly symmetrical.

**XVI — The Tower**
A gold tower built from stacked geometric shapes (rectangles, trapezoids) splits down the center with a jagged lightning bolt of electric blue — the only non-gold element. Two small figures fall from opposite sides, rendered as simple geometric silhouettes with flowing Art Deco robes trailing upward. Crown fragments (triangles and circles) scatter in the space above the tower. Flames rendered as repeating chevron patterns emanate from the tower's crown. The base sits on a rocky outcrop made of interlocking triangles. The background is pure black — the destruction feels absolute.

**XVII — The Star**
A kneeling figure pours water from two golden vessels — one onto land (a geometric terrain of hexagons), one into a pool (concentric circles). Above: one large eight-pointed star surrounded by seven smaller stars, all in gold with radiating line burst patterns. A bird (ibis-like, angular, geometric) perches on a single tree branch in the upper right. The figure's body is composed of flowing curves — the most organic form in the deck — contrasting with the geometric environment. The water streams are parallel gold lines flowing with grace. The scene feels like an oasis of hope in the geometric universe.

### Typography on Cards
- **Card name:** Cinzel, 500 weight, `#D4AF37` gold, ALL CAPS, letter-spacing 0.15em, centered in top zone
- **Card number:** Cormorant Garamond, 400 italic, gold, small, below card name
- **Size:** Card name at 32px, number at 18px

### Card Back Design
Pure black background. Central motif: an eight-pointed star made of intersecting gold lines, surrounded by a circle of 12 zodiac glyphs evenly spaced. The "OA" monogram sits at the center of the star. Thin gold border, 2px from edges. Perfectly symmetrical — the card back should be mesmerizing to stare at.

## Deck 2: "Celestial Watercolor" (The Soft Arcana)

### Concept
Dreamy watercolor illustrations with soft edges, pastel washes, and gold foil accents. Each card feels like a page from a hand-painted mystical journal. Figures are ethereal, semi-transparent, blending into their cosmic surroundings. The aesthetic is romantic and feminine without being childish.

**Art direction:** Watercolor illustration style — visible brush strokes, soft color bleeds, paper texture. Color palette: soft lavenders, peach, dusty rose, sage green, with gold metallic accents for stars and borders. Figures have delicate features, flowing hair, loose clothing. Backgrounds are abstract cosmic watercolor washes.

**Brand alignment:** Variant B (Ethereal Dawn)

### Major Arcana Samples

**0 — The Fool**
A young woman with flowing auburn hair walks barefoot along a cliff edge painted in soft peach and sage watercolor. She wears a loose, flowing dress in lavender that bleeds into the sky around her — she is almost becoming the sky itself. In one hand she carries a small bundle tied to a wildflower stem. A small white fox (not a dog — Olivia's unique touch) walks beside her, partially transparent like a spirit guide. The cliff is rendered in warm terracotta washes. Above, a soft golden sun painted as a metallic foil circle with watercolor rays bleeding outward. Butterflies (3-4, in dusty rose) float around her head. The overall feeling is whimsical courage — stepping into the unknown with grace.

**II — The High Priestess**
A figure sits on a silver-blue crescent moon hovering over still water painted in lavender and indigo washes. She wears deep blue robes that cascade down like waterfalls, blending into the water below. In her lap: an open book with pages that appear to glow with soft gold light. Behind her: a veil that is literally a wash of watercolor — abstract purple and blue bleeding into the background, suggesting mystery without depicting a literal veil. Her eyes are closed, her expression peaceful. Pomegranates hang from delicate vines framing the top of the card — painted in dusty rose with gold seeds visible. The moon she sits on has a metallic gold foil surface.

**XVI — The Tower**
A tower made of pale stone watercolor washes cracks apart — the crack rendered as raw, unpainted white paper showing through. Lightning is a slash of gold foil cutting diagonally across the card. Two figures fall — but they're dissolving into watercolor splashes as they descend, their forms breaking into drops of color (rose, lavender, amber). Rain falls as delicate watercolor dots. The sky above is a stormy wash of charcoal and deep purple, but at the very bottom, through the crack, a small patch of sunrise peach is visible — destruction reveals light beneath.

**XVII — The Star**
The most beautiful card in the deck. A figure kneels by a pool that reflects the night sky — the reflection is a watercolor galaxy of soft purples, blues, and silver. She pours water from a gold vessel; the water is depicted as flowing gold foil that becomes watercolor blue as it touches the pool. Eight stars in the sky are gold foil circles with soft watercolor glow halos in different colors (one per visible color of the rainbow, plus one pure white). Wildflowers in sage and peach grow at the pool's edge. The figure's skin has a subtle golden shimmer. The composition radiates peace and renewal.

### Card Back Design
Soft lavender background with subtle paper texture. Central motif: a watercolor moon surrounded by a wreath of wildflowers (roses, lavender, baby's breath) in soft pinks and greens. Zodiac symbols are faintly visible in the background in dusty mauve. The "OA" monogram in warm amber sits below the moon. Gold foil dots are scattered like stars across the entire back. Edges have a soft, irregular watercolor border (not a hard line).

## Deck 3: "Neon Oracle" (The Electric Arcana)

### Concept
Cyberpunk-meets-cosmos. Dark backgrounds with neon glow outlines. Think Blade Runner fortune teller. The figures are rendered in glowing electric outlines — cyan, magenta, violet — against void-black backgrounds. Everything has a soft neon glow bloom. Ultra-modern, breaks every tarot tradition.

**Art direction:** Neon line art with glow effects on black. Color palette: electric cyan, hot magenta, ultra violet, with white core glow lines. Figures are stylized with clean line work — like neon signs in human form. Backgrounds have subtle grid lines (cyberpunk aesthetic) and distant neon star fields.

**Brand alignment:** Variant C (Mystic Indigo)

### Major Arcana Samples

**0 — The Fool**
A figure outlined in electric cyan neon stands at the edge of a digital cliff — the cliff face rendered as a wireframe grid fading into darkness below. They wear a hooded jacket outlined in magenta glow, one foot already over the edge. In their hand: a small glowing sphere of pure white light (not a star — a data orb). Behind them: a neon dog (more wolf-like) in violet outline, baring its teeth playfully. The sky is pure black with horizontal scan lines (CRT effect). Three floating neon triangles orbit the figure's head like a glitching halo. At the very top, a neon sun: a circle of cyan with radiating lines that fade to magenta at their tips.

**II — The High Priestess**
A figure seated on a throne of floating geometric shapes — cubes, pyramids, spheres — all outlined in faint violet neon. She is outlined in cool white with cyan accents. Two vertical neon bars (B and J pillars) flank her in magenta and cyan respectively. Her "scroll" is a floating holographic screen displaying scrolling constellation data (represented as horizontal lines of varying lengths). Behind her: a curtain of vertical neon lines in gradient from violet to cyan, creating a digital veil. Her crown: a WiFi-like signal arc symbol that suggests transmission/reception of cosmic data. The overall feel: she is the cosmic database, and she's granting you access.

**XVI — The Tower**
A skyscraper-shaped tower rendered in cold white neon wireframe, shattering into fragments — each fragment trailing magenta and cyan particle effects as it flies outward. A massive lightning bolt in pure white with magenta glow strikes from above. Two figures eject in opposite directions, their outlines glitching and fragmenting (some body parts are displaced, VHS-distortion style). The crown of the tower explodes into a burst of neon sparks. The background has a subtle matrix-style rain of tiny cyan characters. At the base: cracks in the ground glow red-orange — the only warm color in the deck, and it means danger.

**XVII — The Star**
A figure kneels, outlined in soft white glow, pouring streams of light from two vessels. The streams are animated-looking: one is cyan dots flowing downward (digital water), the other is magenta particles floating upward (digital fire/energy). Above: eight neon stars, each a different color of the spectrum, arranged in a circle. The central star is larger, pulsing (depicted with concentric glow rings). The ground is a reflective black mirror surface showing star reflections. A small neon bird (hummingbird shape, in green — the only green in the deck) hovers near the water. The feeling: hope as a signal broadcasting into the void.

- **Card name:** Space Grotesk, 700 bold, `#00D4FF` electric cyan with subtle glow, ALL CAPS, centered
- **Card number:** IBM Plex Sans, 400, `#C77DFF` bright orchid, small
- **Size:** Card name at 34px, number at 16px

### Card Back Design
Pure black with a central sacred geometry pattern (Metatron's cube) rendered in neon lines — cyan outline, violet inner connections, magenta core points. The pattern pulses with subtle glow gradients. The "OA" monogram in white neon at center. Outer border: a thin cyan neon line with soft glow. Tiny neon dots scattered like stars. The overall effect: a glowing mystical circuit board.

## Deck 4: "Terra Mystica" (The Ancient Arcana)

### Concept
Woodcut-inspired illustrations with a medieval manuscript feel. Think 15th-century Marseilles tarot reimagined with modern composition but historical printing techniques. Limited color palette: black ink, oxblood red, forest green, aged gold. Crosshatching for shading. The most traditional-looking deck, but with refined modern proportions.

**Art direction:** Block print / woodcut illustration style. Heavy black outlines, crosshatch shading, flat color fills in a limited palette. Figures are depicted in classical poses from historical tarot. Paper texture is prominent — the cards look printed on aged parchment.

**Brand alignment:** Variant D (Terracotta Oracle)

### Major Arcana Samples

**0 — The Fool**
A traveler in medieval clothing (tunic, hose, feathered cap) walks rightward toward a cliff edge. The figure is rendered in heavy black ink outlines with crosshatch shading on the clothing folds. A small dog bites at the hem of the tunic. The traveler carries a bindle (stick with bundle) over the right shoulder. The sun in the upper left is rendered as a woodcut face with radiating lines. The cliff is depicted with sharp crosshatch lines suggesting rocky texture. A single flower (rose, in oxblood red) grows at the cliff edge. The border is a thick black line with corner ornaments in the style of medieval manuscript decoration. Background: aged parchment with no color — just ink and paper.

**II — The High Priestess**
A robed figure sits facing forward on a stone throne between two pillars rendered in heavy crosshatch. She wears a Papal tiara (traditional Papess imagery). In her lap, an open book with visible text lines (tiny crosshatch marks suggesting text). Behind her: a tapestry curtain rendered as a dense crosshatch pattern with pomegranate motifs visible. The crescent moon at her feet is one of the few areas of aged gold color. Her robes flow in heavy, sculptural folds with meticulous crosshatch shading. The overall image looks like it could have been pulled from a Nuremberg Chronicle page.

**XVI — The Tower**
The most dramatic card. A stone tower (medieval fortress style with crenellations) is struck by a jagged lightning bolt rendered as a thick black zigzag with a gold fill. Two figures fall headfirst from the tower — their bodies in dynamic poses with robes and hair streaming upward. Flames (in oxblood red) burst from the tower windows and crown. Falling stones are rendered as small crosshatched blocks scattered around the composition. Rain depicted as thin vertical lines. The sky behind is solid crosshatch suggesting dark storm clouds. This is the most ink-heavy, densely illustrated card in the deck.

**XVII — The Star**
A nude figure kneels by a stream, pouring water from two jugs — one into the stream (rendered as flowing crosshatch lines), one onto the ground where it feeds growing plants. Above: one large eight-pointed star in aged gold, surrounded by seven smaller stars in black outline. An ibis perches on a tree in the background — the tree rendered in detailed crosshatch with individual leaves suggested by tiny marks. The landscape is gentle rolling hills with crosshatch shading to suggest distance. The figure's body is rendered with delicate crosshatch that suggests soft skin against the harder textures of the environment. The most serene composition in the deck.

- **Card number:** Cormorant, 400 italic, `#B8963E` aged gold
- **Size:** Card name at 28px, number at 16px

### Card Back Design
Aged parchment background. Central motif: a woodcut-style mandala composed of interlocking zodiac animals and symbols in a circular arrangement. Heavy black ink rendering. A single aged gold star at the center with the "OA" monogram. Border: thick black line with Celtic-knot-inspired corner decorations. The back looks like a page from a medieval bestiary.

## Deck 5: "Astral Minimal" (The Clean Arcana)

### Concept
Minimal line art with vast negative space. Each card has ONE focal element rendered in clean, thin black lines on white, with a single accent color per card (drawn from the zodiac element: fire=red, water=blue, air=yellow, earth=green). The figures are simplified to essential forms — like architectural drawings of people. The most visually distinct deck: it looks nothing like traditional tarot, which is the point.

**Art direction:** Thin monoline illustration (1-2px consistent line weight), generous white space (60%+ empty), single accent color per card, no textures, no gradients, no shading. Figures are geometric simplifications — a face is an oval with two dots for eyes. The deck is meant to feel like a meditation: calm, focused, essential.

**Brand alignment:** Variant E (Minimal Cosmos)

### Major Arcana Samples

**0 — The Fool**
A simple figure — oval head, straight-line body, triangular dress/tunic shape — stands with one foot beyond a clean horizontal line (the cliff). The figure is pure black line art. A tiny circle (the sun) sits in the upper right quadrant of the card with 8 thin radiating lines. A small geometric dog shape (triangle body, circle head) sits at the figure's heel. The accent color for this card: a single thin vertical line in `#4A3AFF` (deep indigo) connecting the figure's heart to the sun — representing the fool's connection to the divine. Everything else is black lines on white. The composition is breathtakingly sparse. 80% of the card is empty white space.

**II — The High Priestess**
A seated figure (geometric: triangle for seated body, oval for head, two lines for arms resting on thighs). Two thin vertical lines flank her (the pillars) — one labeled B, one J, in the smallest possible type. Across her lap: a thin horizontal line (the scroll). Behind her: a field of tiny dots in a regular grid pattern (the veil of knowledge, reduced to data). The accent color: a single crescent moon shape in `#4A3AFF` deep indigo, floating above her head. That crescent is the only curved element in an otherwise angular composition.

**XVI — The Tower**
A tall thin rectangle (the tower) with a diagonal crack splitting it. Two small figure shapes (oval + line body) are depicted mid-fall on either side of the tower, at slight angles. A single zigzag line (lightning) comes from the top of the card. The accent color: the interior of the crack is filled with `#E8524A` (mars red) — the ONLY fill in the entire deck. No other card has a filled shape. This makes The Tower visually alarming in a deck that is otherwise pure line art. Three small circles (debris) float around the tower.

**XVII — The Star**
A kneeling figure (simplified: bent lines for legs, oval for torso, small circle for head) holds two thin lines angled downward (pouring water). Below: a horizontal line with small wave marks (the pool). Above: one large eight-pointed star made of 4 overlapping lines, centered, plus 7 small dots arranged in a loose arc. The accent color: all water lines and the pool are in `#4A3AFF` deep indigo. A single thin line connects the largest star to the figure's head. This card is the most open, the most white space, the most peaceful. It's practically empty — and that emptiness IS the message.

### Typography on Cards
- **Card name:** Inter, 600, `#1A1A1A` near black, ALL CAPS, letter-spacing 0.15em, centered
- **Card number:** Inter, 400, `#888888` mid gray
- **Size:** Card name at 28px, number at 14px

### Card Back Design
Pure white background. Central motif: a single thin-line circle with 12 equally spaced dots on its circumference (zodiac positions). A tiny "OA" in the center, Inter font, mid gray. Nothing else. The most minimal card back possible — it should look like it belongs in a museum of modern art.
