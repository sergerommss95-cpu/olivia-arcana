## What Olivia *has to be* regardless of direction

Grounded in what the brand already says:
- **She's the writer behind every reading.** The bio will say so: *"I've been reading charts for fifteen years…"* The avatar has to look like someone who would actually write those words.
- **Libra sun · Cancer moon · Sagittarius rising · Edinburgh.** Her chart is in the bio. Her look should be *compatible* with Libra sun (refined, balanced) + Sagittarius rising (warm, outward-reaching).
- **Not campy. Not woo-woo.** The site voice is *editorial cosmic almanac*, not crystal shop. Olivia is closer to a *New Yorker* columnist than to a tarot-shop owner.
- **Palette-compatible.** The whole site is cosmic void `#06041a` + gold `#D4AF37` + violet. Whatever she's wearing and shot against should live inside that palette.
- **Age 30–45.** 15 years of reading charts means she started at 20. Late-20s to mid-40s is honest.
- **Female presence.** "Olivia" is the name, and every sign on the site points to a woman. Not a hard rule — but this is the bet.

## HeyGen-specific technical brief (applies to whichever direction)

Whichever portrait we end up with, for HeyGen to produce a convincing talking avatar, the **source image must meet these requirements** or the lip-sync / expression blending will look off:

| Requirement | Why |
|---|---|
| **Neutral frontal or ≤15° 3/4 angle** | HeyGen's facial rig maps cleanly at near-front angles. More than 20° off-axis and the lip-sync drifts. |
| **Even light on the face** | Shadows on the face are baked into the source and will stay as Olivia speaks. Rim light and backlight are fine. |
| **Mouth: neutral-closed or very slight smile** | A wide open smile on the source image will "break" when HeyGen tries to articulate consonants. |
| **Hair off the face** | Nothing crossing lips, eyes, or the forehead line. No bangs over brows. |
| **1024×1024 minimum, 2048×2048 ideal** | HeyGen upscales but better to start clean. |
| **Direct camera eye contact** | Off-axis gaze breaks viewer trust for talking-head content. |
| **No hands visible near the face** | HeyGen won't animate them, and they'll look frozen while the face moves. |
| **Single consistent light temperature** | Mixed lighting (e.g. warm key + cool fill) confuses the avatar's real-time relighting. |

**Optional post-portrait step**: once you have a portrait picked, shoot or generate **2–3 expression variants** (neutral, slight smile, thoughtful) — HeyGen can use these as blend targets for more expressive speech.

## Questions to narrow before we generate

1. **Which of the four directions is closest?** If none, what's off?
2. **Age**: is 30–45 right, or are you imagining older/younger?
3. **Accent**: is the Edinburgh detail real to the bio or decorative? If real, Direction 3's UK voice fits naturally. If decorative, neutral American is safer for US audiences.
4. **Ethnicity**: leave open to what Midjourney/Flux generates, or steer specifically? (My default: leave open, choose the most distinctive of 4 candidates.)
5. **Photorealistic or stylized-illustration?** HeyGen requires photorealistic. If you ever want an illustrated version too (for the OliviaIntro card, separate from the talking avatar), that's a second commission.
6. **When do you want to record voice?** Voice first or image first — doesn't matter which, but lock one before the other so they match in tone.
