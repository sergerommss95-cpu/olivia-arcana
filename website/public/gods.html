Olivia Arcana · the final round

Five marks seeking one identity

Twenty-two concepts narrowed by cross-check against four criteria: ownable, legible at any scale, virally storyable, brand-congruent. These are the five that passed all four. Now the work is to look at each long enough to know which is not just good — but inevitable.

I · etymology

The Olive

Olivia means "olive tree" in Latin. The brand's own name made visible — a Fibonacci-spiraling olive branch with three ripe fruits reaching toward a single star. Athena's gift to her city. Peace, wisdom, sacred continuity. The most ownable mark in the set: no competitor can claim this back-story retroactively.

II · sacred geometry

The Wheel of Seven

Sacred geometry inside the astrological instrument. Seven Seed-of-Life circles counter-rotating against twelve zodiac stations, a single planet traversing the outer ring, the ✦ holding still at center. The Seed's six outer circles align with six of the twelve zodiac positions — a geometric coincidence the viewer subconsciously reads. Product (Wheel) holds meaning (Seed); the star is the reader.

III · constellation

The Pleiades

M45 — the Seven Sisters. The oldest feminine asterism across every recorded culture: Greek, Aztec, Maori, Vedic, Ancestral Puebloan. Seven brightest stars named; an eighth, Alcor, barely visible as a whisper. The cluster's real astronomical coordinates are printed like a catalogue entry. A tarot brand for a woman named Olivia, signed with the sky's oldest sorority.

IV · figurative

The Diviner

Give the brand a face. Classical Greco-Roman female profile — hair in a traditional bun, gaze lifted to a single star. A contour drawing in the vein of Picasso's line portraits or the silhouettes on ancient coinage. Vates — the Latin word for seer, used in Virgil and Ovid. The brand becomes a figure; the figure becomes iconic.

V · mythic

The Labyrinth

The Cretan 7-circuit labyrinth — carved on coins of Knossos c. 400 BC, the oldest divinatory symbol in Western culture. A single unbroken path: one way in, one way out, the oracle at center. Not a maze (no choices); a ritual. The deepest visual metaphor for a tarot reading compressed to a silhouette that works at any scale.

Each card is rendered at 480 × 720 — the ratio of the physical deck. Motion is choreographed, not layered: each composition has one signature gesture. Reduced-motion users see the static composition. Each mark works as the primary brand identity, the app icon, the favicon, and the card back — without modification. Scroll up, scroll down, take your time. The answer is here.
