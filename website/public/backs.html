✦ Olivia Arcana

Twenty-two backs of the deck

Twenty-two directions — the same number as the Major Arcana. The last five (XVIII–XXII) are built for virality: each has a brand-native story someone wants to tell. "Did you know Olivia means olive tree?" "Their logo is Athena's own owl." "It's the oldest labyrinth in the West." A logo without a story is forgotten. A logo with one spreads.

I · distilled

The Eye

The original idea, stripped clean. Just the almond, the iris, the ✦ at its heart — nothing between the viewer and the seer. The blink is the only event. Corner diamond markers anchor the composition without pulling focus.

II · singular

The Star

No eye, no ornament, no Latin. A single radiant ✦ floating inside two counter-rotating orbit rings, with a planet-dot tracing the outer orbit. The brand is the four-point star. Patek Philippe restraint — confidence through absence.

III · astrological

The Wheel

Twelve zodiac stations around a central ✦. Roman numerals mark the four cardinal angles (ASC / IC / DSC / MC). The most brand-native option: an astrology product whose back is the instrument of astrology. Reads as editorial-almanac.

IV · nocturnal

The Nocturne

A real constellation — Ursa Major, drawn to accurate relative positions with proper star labels and RA/DEC coordinates. The most editorial option: tells a story before the card is even flipped. Could rotate through 22 different constellations across the deck, each card's back chosen by the user's birth chart.

V · haute couture

The Maison

Fashion-house monogram. A large italic "O" in Cormorant Garamond with the ✦ mark set inside, "ARCANA" in wide caps below, "MAISON · MMXXVI" subscript. Reads as a luxury house, not a mystical brand. Premium signal without a single esoteric symbol.

VI · radiant

The Third Eye

Vertical vesica piscis — ancient mandorla shape, symbol of intersection between worlds. The ✦ sits in the center as pure radiance; slow gold rays emanate outward. Strong mystical weight without falling into the surveillance-eye read of the horizontal almond. Reads as "revelation," not "observation."

VII · precision

The Aperture

A camera iris drawn in engineering-hairline geometry, with the light-through-the-aperture as a single dot. The mechanical truth of "Olivia sees" — no mysticism, just optics. Six blades, one reference ring, three construction lines, one central dot. Nothing else. Machined.

VIII · editorial

The Ephemeris

A single catalogued star, rendered as editorial type. Object name in eyebrow caps, common name in italic display, coordinates and magnitude as sub-data. One gold dot marks the fix. Feels like a page of The Monocle Astronomy Issue. The card back as journal entry.

IX · navigation

The Compass

Mariner's compass rose — the navigator's instrument rather than the astrologer's wheel. 4 cardinal arrows, 4 intercardinal outlines, 12 tick marks. A micro-needle rotates slowly, always searching. Classical navigation precision: the deck as a tool for finding your bearing.

X · geometric

The Seed

Seed of Life: seven circles arranged in classical construction, drawn at pure 0.5pt hairline weight. Central ✦ as the generative point. Ancient sacred geometry with zero mysticism — just the mathematics of the hexagonal close-packing. Could be drawn with a compass on vellum. The cosmos as geometry.

XI · maison

The Signet

No frame. No symbol. Just the brand name set as three lines of precise type: eyebrow, italic display Olivia, wide caps ARCANA, single signet mark, datum. The entire design is typography at exact proportions. Patek Philippe dial: if the name is enough, everything else is noise.

XII · horizon

The Horizon

Absolute reduction. One horizon line, one sun half-risen, one timestamp. Everything the card needs to say — "the moment light returns" — said with 4 elements. Minimalist cinema in gold. The card back as a single frame of a sunrise caught at exactly 06h 47m.

XIII · constellation

The Pleiades

A real stellar cluster — M45, the Seven Sisters — drawn to the actual relative positions of its seven visible stars. The Pleiades is the oldest feminine asterism in almost every culture (Greek, Aztec, Maori, Chinese, Vedic). Ancient and specific. Unforgettable silhouette. AI products don't claim this — they claim the generic ✦.

XIV · celestial

The Orbit

One ellipse. One central body. One traveling dot. That's it. The orbit is the universal symbol of the moving heavens — pure astronomy, pre-dating any digital sparkle by four thousand years. The dot is today, the ellipse is your cycle. Functions as favicon, loading spinner, and app-icon without modification. Distinctly not AI.

XV · handwriting

The Quill

The brand's tagline is literally "real cosmic guidance, written by hand." So make that the sigil. A feathered quill with an ink drop — zero ambiguity about what the brand promises. Deliberate anti-AI statement: this is the one tool a machine can't hold. Latin scripta manu (written by hand) at the top seals the positioning.

XVI · natal

The Ascendant

A simplified natal chart: horizontal Asc-Dsc axis, dotted vertical MC-IC axis, single diagonal line at the birth angle. The most brand-specific direction — the card back literally is the product's output, stripped to its essential geometry. Not decorative: technical. The chart a real astrologer draws when they sit down with your birth data.

✦ XVII · hero

The Wheel of Seven

Rota septemque — the Wheel of Seven. Sacred geometry nested inside the astrological instrument. Seven Seed-of-Life circles counter-rotate against the twelve zodiac stations, a single planet traverses the outer ring, and the ✦ holds still at the center of it all. The Seed's six outer circles align with six of the twelve zodiac positions — a geometric coincidence the design quietly exploits. Every element earns its place: the product (the Wheel) holds the meaning (the Seed), and the star is the reader at the center.

XVIII · etymology

The Olive

Olivia is Latin for olive tree. Athena's gift to the city that bore her name. Make the brand's own etymology visible: an olive branch with leaves and fruit reaching toward a single star. The most ownable sigil in the set — no one else can name a brand after an olive tree retroactively. Unforgettable silhouette. Virality: "Did you know Olivia's logo is literally her name?"

XIX · wisdom

The Owl

Athena's owl — the oldest Western symbol of wisdom, night, and divination. A geometric owl with two luminous eyes perched on a single branch, watching. Noctua Minervæ — the owl of Minerva, which flies only at dusk. Iconic silhouette. Recognizable at 16px. No AI tool claims this — they're too young to.

XX · mythic

The Labyrinth

The Cretan labyrinth — oldest divinatory symbol in Western culture, 4,000+ years. A single path in, a single path out, with the oracle at the center. A tiny gold walker traces the arc; the viewer's eye follows. No dead-ends, no choices — just a winding inevitable journey to the center. The deepest metaphor for a tarot reading compressed to a single recognizable silhouette.

XXI · chronological

The Hourglass of Heavens

An hourglass whose sand is stars. Time and cosmos compressed into one unambiguous symbol. The narrow neck is "your moment" — the hour the stars fell. Most visually unusual of all the variants; most instantly meme-able. Universal visual language (time / heavens) with a twist (the grains are constellations).

XXII · figurative

The Diviner

Give the brand a face. Classical Greco-Roman female profile — hair in a traditional bun, gaze lifted to a single star. The most personal of all the variants; the card back becomes a portrait of Olivia herself. A line drawing in the vein of Picasso's profiles, or the silhouettes on coinage. Becomes the brand's signature figure across every touchpoint.
