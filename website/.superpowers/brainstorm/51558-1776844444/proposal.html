The Daily — a two-minute editorial ritual, anchored to your real sky

Not an experience. A habit. Something you open every morning and close with something specific for your day.

Why this — the reasoning before the design

"what did I learn?" — beats — "what did I see?"

The site ranked 15/100 because it was beautiful with nothing to take away. Users closed it with nothing for themselves. The top astrology/tarot products — Co-Star, Chani, The Pattern — are all ugly but daily habits that deliver specific personal insight. Users return because they get something.

The opportunity: be both. Editorial-magazine beautiful and specific-daily-utility. Nobody in the space owns that intersection. Today's editorial sites (Chani web) are pretty but shallow; today's real tools (Astro-Seek) are functional but brutal.

This direction pairs a real, novel utility — a reading from YOUR current sky — with print-magazine craft. It's the one combination that doesn't exist yet.

AESTHETIC

The Gentlewoman × MoMA exhibition catalog. Print-quality typography, full-bleed card photography, earned whitespace. Zero "web animation tricks."

VALUE

A 2-minute ritual. One card pulled from YOUR current sky. One reading specific to this moment. One concrete ritual action for today.

USEFUL

Daily habit. Personal (location + time + optional birth). Archived (your month builds a record). Shareable (auto-generated image per reading).

The user flow — under two minutes end to end

T+0
Arrival
Near-black page. One line: "good morning. Reykjavík, 07:42." Subtle sunrise gradient at the horizon below the fold.
T+3s
The moment
Massive editorial headline — today's cosmic thesis in one sentence. "A tender patience is the day's medicine. The moon draws slowly full." 60-90px italic serif.
T+8s
The card
Scroll. Full-bleed real v2.7 card on the left half (e.g. The Hermit). Right half: card name in 70px serif, 2 sentences of reading specific to this moment and your optional chart, and one concrete action.
T+25s
Your sky
Scroll. Clean scientific sky diagram (SVG-drawn, museum quality) — current planet positions at your location. Small italic annotations: "Venus ⚻ Saturn · 14°32′ — a pause, not a stop."
T+45s
The ritual
Scroll. A single concrete action for today in 28px italic serif, framed like a printed instruction card. "Write one letter you won't send. Seal it. The Hermit keeps."
T+70s
Share · save · deeper
Scroll. Four understated links: save to your record · share this reading · browse the deck · book a real reading. No signup required; cookie-based archive grows locally or syncs if user signs up.

What makes this actually useful (not ornamental)

• Real ephemeris calculation. Current planet positions are real, pulled from astronomy-engine (MIT-licensed library). Not "mystical illustration."

• Reading is deterministic by your sky, not random. Same moment + same location = same card. If you add birth info, it personalizes via transits-to-natal aspects.

• Shareable artifact — auto-generated poster (Open Graph image) per reading. Friends clicking the link see your reading preserved.

• Local archive. Every reading you see stays in your browser. After 30 days, a pattern emerges — your personal cosmic record.

• Bridge to the real product. Deck browsing + physical deck purchase + human reading booking are one tap away, always.
