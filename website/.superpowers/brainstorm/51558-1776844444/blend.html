1

The Sky Is the Deck

the 78 cards live IN the sky — pluck a card like plucking a star
ARRIVAL Geolocation → the live current sky above you fills the viewport. Real stars. Real planets at their current positions. Moon at its current phase. Quiet and dark.
CORE MECHANIC: all 78 Olivia Arcana cards are distributed across the sky, each anchored to an astrological region (zodiac sign × modality). Current transiting planets cast light onto specific cards — those cards glow faintly, drift forward. Cards outside current transits sit farther back, dimmer.
  • Drag to pan the sky. Scroll zooms in/out.
  • Hover a card — it floats to the viewer, tilts, shows its face.
  • Click to pull: card rises out of the sky, flips, reveals the reading with a note: "drawn from the 9th house of Sagittarius, while Jupiter transits."
  • Pull 3 cards for a full spread. Each flagged by where in the sky it came from.
ProsCompletely signature. One-line pitch: "the sky above you is the deck." Every visitor's sky is different. Massive Awwwards ceiling.
ConsMost technically ambitious (real astronomy math + 78 cards in 3D + interaction). Most time to build right.
2

The Reading from Now

the sky reads for you — an hourly oracle, shareable as a card
ARRIVAL A single line: "The sky is reading for you. [time at your location]." Three cards float down from the sky, pre-selected by the current transits. The sky is visible but subtle — the CARDS and the reading are centerstage.
CORE MECHANIC: every hour, the transiting planets choose 3 cards for the collective. Everyone who visits in the same hour gets the same 3 cards (a shared cosmic event), but the reading is contextualized to the viewer's local sunrise/sunset. Tomorrow at 3pm, totally different cards. Six times a day the world's reading changes.
  • Three cards centerstage with the sky as backdrop.
  • Each card labeled: "Venus → The Star · Mars → The Tower · Moon → The Priestess"
  • Shareable: permalink + auto-generated image of your hour's spread.
  • Option: enter birth info → get a personal layer on top of the collective one.
ProsViral mechanic (shared hourly spreads = Twitter-style cohort). Easier build. Returns daily ("what did the sky say today?"). Less 3D complexity.
ConsLess exploration — you receive the reading rather than pull one. Sky is context, not centerpiece.
3

The Observatory — navigable tool

multiple modes: live sky, your chart, the full deck, the rituals
ARRIVAL Dark viewport with four anchors visible in the distance: The Sky · The Chart · The Deck · The Rooms. Camera can fly between them in 3D. Each is its own destination.
CORE MECHANIC: the site is an instrument with 4 modes navigable via camera fly-through (no page navigation).
  • Sky mode — current sky above your location (from B)
  • Chart mode — input birth info, see your personal natal chart as a 3D space
  • Deck mode — all 78 cards fanned in 3D, pick any (from A)
  • Rooms mode — paid ritual rooms (from earlier scope)
Camera transitions between modes feel like traveling through cosmic space. Each mode is independently rich.
ProsMost product-complete. Everything lives in one experience. Supports the previous scope (rituals, chart). Tool people return to.
ConsLeast focused signature. Four modes = four things to polish. Risk of feeling like a "cosmic Framer template."
