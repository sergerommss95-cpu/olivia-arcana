Pick the exact flavor

All three deliver the daily utility inside a cinematic dark scene. They differ in how alive, how immersive, and how much the user can do.

1

The Living Observatory

one cinematic live scene · real sky · the card rises from it · scroll zooms the camera

You land inside a dark cinematic sky. Planets are at their actual current positions, drawn as glowing glyphs over a slow-drifting shader nebula and a star field. As the scene settles, today's card rises from the sky center — real v2.7 artwork, tilted, lit from within, with hairline gold lines drawing from it to the planets that chose it. A typewritten reading appears beside it. Scroll advances the camera (zoom in for the card, pull back for the aspects, down for the ritual). The whole site is one living scene.

Aesthetic: Death Stranding + Kurzgesagt + Stellarium. Utility: same as the magazine (real time/place/cards/aspects/ritual). Signature: the sky is literally your sky, and the card is literally drawn from it.

2

The Altar Under the Sky

a dark room with a lit altar — the sky is visible through a skylight above

You see a low dark foreground: a stone altar with today's real card placed upon it, a single candle flame. Above: the real live sky visible through an architectural opening. The card is the focal object; the sky is the atmosphere. Planetary glyphs drift above. Reading appears beside the altar. Ritual below. More chamber, less cosmos. Feels like entering a specific place at the same time every day.

Aesthetic: Kojima chapel + Tarkovsky interior. Different from 1: lower, closer, more architectural. Risk: less "wow" entry than 1.

3

The Living Depth — interactive 3D sky

the whole site is a navigable 3D cosmos · drag to rotate · scroll is camera dolly

Like 1, but the user has real agency. The sky is a navigable 3D space. Drag to rotate the celestial sphere around you. Today's card floats in the middle; orbit around it. Planets you pass near pop with their current aspect. Scroll is camera dolly (not page scroll). Full-spectacle, most Awwwards-y, highest build complexity. Risk: users might get lost / not know what to do.

Aesthetic: Bruno Simon Folio × Olivia Arcana. Utility: exploration tool. Signature: you can fly through your own sky.
